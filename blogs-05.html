Augmented Reality in Education: Benefits and Modern Use Cases

Perhaps, Education is one of the most beneficial sectors of augmented reality innovations. Augmented reality has revolutionized classrooms into whole new dimensions. Long lectures that make everyone sleepy are no longer necessary! Do you want Fun along with education and training? bring AR into your classforms !

An ordinary class can become an engaging experience with Augmented Reality in education apps. The proverb says, “Tell me and I’ll forget, show me and I’ll remember, involve me and I’ll understand.” This sums up the role of AR in education.

Traditional educational approaches as we once knew them are fading into history. They are being influenced by technological advancements and becoming more and more digital. The industry of “Ed-Tech,” or education technology, is predicted to reach $680.1 billion by 2027, expanding at a rate of 17.9% annually.

Augmented reality is without a doubt one of the most important topics in ed-tech. With the value of AR in ed-tech expected to hit $5.3 billion by 2023, there will undoubtedly be new opportunities for corporations and educational institutions.

There are several uses for augmented reality in education. It makes it simpler for the pupils to learn, understand, and retain the material. Additionally, AR enhances the fun and engagement of learning itself. Furthermore, it is not restricted to any one age group or educational level and may be applied successfully at all academic levels, including pre-school, college, and even the workplace.

The use of augmented reality (AR) in education includes elements that improve students’ learning of skills including teamwork, problem-solving, and creativity. It is also beneficial for conventional schooling that emphasizes technical expertise and knowledge.

Even while augmented reality (AR) is still in its infancy, especially in comparison to virtual reality (VR), it does give educational institutions with limited budgets more affordable options while still offering many of the same features and advantages.

Challenges of Augmented Reality in Education

Augmented reality has no doubt completely changed technological trends in education and will continue to do further. Aside from the advantages, there are a few challenges related to using such learning resources:

The right digital equipment is needed

On outdated phone models or those with OS versions that do not enable immersion, an AR application cannot be installed. The purchase of smartphones or smart glasses may be out of the question because schools are not often known for having the most cutting-edge technological equipment.

The lack of teachers’ experience with tech

Some educators do not employ or even accept contemporary technologies. When the teacher is required to demonstrate how the device functions and assist students in case of problems, this could be a problem.

Overall, there are a lot more advantages to employing augmented reality applications for education than drawbacks. Additionally, the aforementioned difficulties can be easily overcome.

Uses of Augmented Reality in Education

Are you considering using augmented reality in education? Applications for augmented reality can be a wonderful solution in a plethora of industries. So let’s look at augmented reality’s potential for usage in education.

Augmented reality in schools

How does AR work in schools? A better depiction of the content being studied can be achieved by using this program during lessons. Schoolchildren, for instance, can engage in a more involved and interesting exploration of such complex subjects as biology, anatomy, physics, and even algebra. A classroom augmented reality app can also replicate many instances that are challenging to explain and even foster children’s inventiveness.

But can you employ augmented reality in a school setting? Many members of the younger age already own personal gadgets, and the majority of contemporary mobile devices, such as smartphones and tablets, are appropriate for use with AR applications. Even though, it’s only advantageous because children can learn whenever they want and finish their schoolwork with greater enthusiasm.

Augmented reality in college

Solutions for augmented reality in higher education can be extremely beneficial for students interested in studying technical or humanitarian sciences. With an AR program that simulates the human body from the inside out, for instance, medical students can learn anatomy and practice doing physical examinations. In addition, many colleges use augmented reality to teach students about astronomy, engineering, arithmetic, and many other subjects.

For pupils, augmented reality makes the learning process more interesting and secure. The use of technology makes it possible to conduct experiments like combining various chemicals to see what happens without endangering the well-being of the students or damaging university property. Additionally, AR applications can aid students in planning university events and engaging in creative campus activities.

AR for distance learning

Due to the epidemic, distant learning has gained tremendous popularity over the past few years. Because of this and other factors, online schooling will probably be popular long after the situation has stabilized. Applications for augmented reality can provide a practical setting for online learning, allowing students to interact in real-time with teachers and other students while still receiving the necessary materials.

We should also point out that students who take classes online don’t necessarily need a teacher to walk them through the subject. Each topic can be covered in-depth in a decent app, along with a concise or in-depth explanation of the theory and interactive exercises. Students can study independently at a high level in their preferred setting.

Augmented reality in-work training

Entry-level specialists and those changing jobs can quickly pick up skills and knowledge by using augmented reality training. For instance, it will enable them to gain a thorough understanding of the theory and working environment without endangering the company or the employee’s health. For instance, training in AR logistics aids in both staff organization and training.

According to research, AR-assisted training increased General Electric employees’ work productivity by 34% and by 46% for GE Healthcare staff.

Augmented Reality Apps for Education

The use of augmented reality by schools and colleges, both on-campus and remote, adds interactivity to existing curricula. Following is a list of augmented reality apps for education:

Human Anatomy Atlas 2021

Students and healthcare professionals can better grasp how the human body functions by using 3D models and simulations of the male and female anatomy. Users can investigate muscle movement, view animations, perform virtual dissections, and more.

Holo-Human

This AR software can examine human anatomy models in a group setting, including interior and 360-degree views. Lesson plans can also be written by teachers.

VR Frog Dissection: Ribbit-ing Discoveries

Students dissect frogs in biology classes all across the world to learn about bodies. This program replaces this method, enabling instructors and students to do virtual dissection studies to learn about frog anatomy.

GeoGebra Augmented Reality

This interactive application facilitates science, technology, engineering, and mathematics (STEM) education using AR elements that let students explore shapes and 3D functions, employ critical thinking skills, and more. It covers everything from geometry and algebra to statistics and calculus.

Expeditions

This versatile tool gives teachers and students the ability to build and experience interactive, virtual settings thanks to its abundance of AR tours.

Exoplanet

This application, built by a qualified astronomer, offers an interactive list of planets that are known to circle stars in the Milky Way. Another astronomy-based AR software is Star Walk, which allows users to view and recognize constellations and stars in real-time while also learning interesting astronomical facts and regular statistics.

Touch Surgery

This app can help doctors and surgeons prepare for surgical cases and learn about different procedures.

4D Interactive Anatomy

Using this 4D interactive anatomy app, students can test their knowledge and faculty can create custom quizzes.

Visible

For students who do not have access to a lab, this app with AR dissection features allows them to see 3D models of human anatomy in real-time.

Boulevard AR

This augmented reality app brings artwork from the National Portrait Gallery to life, giving users a museum-like experience from anywhere.

3DBear

This app combines augmented reality, virtual reality, 360-degree photos, scanning, and 3D printing to improve remote and in-person learning.

zSpace

This augmented reality platform enables faculty to create immersive academic experiences and content for a variety of courses.
